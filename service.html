Admission Tips

What you should know about the admission process

Instead of worrying about meeting a specific set of criteria, try to create an application that will help the school see your achievements — inside the classroom and out — in their true context, so the school can understand your potential to take advantage of the resources at your target school and the kind of contribution you would make to the school community. Show them what kind of student you are. Show them that you have taken advantage of your past experiences, how you have achieved and contributed in your own particular context.

We look for students who make a difference in their schools and communities, so tell us about your leadership activities, interests, special skills and other extracurricular involvements.

Cover Letter

How to write kick ass cover letter

When you look at the job offer, ask yourself: “Do I really want this program?” Rate your answer on a scale from 1 to 10. If your motivation is below 8, do not apply. When you found an 8 or above, you can think about why you are applying. The main purpose of the cover letter is to reassure the recruiter about your ability to perform at this position, to convey that your deep purpose and values are aligned with the position and the company. Resume

All you need to know about your CV

Most CVs include the following sections: Contact information; Academic history; Professional experience; Qualifications and skills; Awards and honors; Publications; Professional associations; Grants and fellowships; Licenses and certificates; Volunteer work; Personal information; Hobbies and interests.

The CV should be clear, concise and easy to read (remember that a conventional CV must fit in 1 page). Use the typo to highlight the information you want the recruiter to grasp quickly (make sure to bold school and company names), make sure that your CV has a logical structure, and list the information in chronological order starting from the most recent
